Expected Value of a Biased Coin Game

Suppose you play a game with a biased coin. You play each game by tossing the coin once. The probability of heads, P(heads), is 2⁄3, and the probability of tails, P(tails), is 1⁄3. If you toss a head, you pay $6. If you toss a tail, you win $10. If you play this game many times, will you come out ahead?
