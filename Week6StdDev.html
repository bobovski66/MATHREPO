Expected Value and Standard Deviation of a Betting Game

In this problem, you’re betting on whether a moderate earthquake will occur over the next 48 hours. The probability of occurrence, P(win), is 21.42%, while the probability of non-occurrence, P(loss), is 78.58%. If you win, you gain $50. If you lose, you pay $20.

Problem:

  1. Calculate the expected value, μ, for this bet.
  2. Use the value of μ to calculate the variance components and standard deviation of the outcomes.

Definitions:

  • Expected Value (μ): The long-term average outcome if you were to repeat this bet many times. It's calculated by summing the products of each possible outcome and its probability.
  • Standard Deviation (σ): A measure of the variation or spread of outcomes around the expected value. A higher standard deviation means more variability in the outcomes.
