Find the distance between the two cities

A satellite measures distances to each city: \(370\,\mathrm{km}\) and \(350\,\mathrm{km}\). The angle at the satellite between those lines of sight is \(2.1^\circ\). What is the distance between the cities?

Solution (Law of Cosines)

Let \(a=370\,\mathrm{km}\), \(b=350\,\mathrm{km}\), and the included angle \(\theta=2.1^\circ\). The distance \(d\) between the cities is the third side of the triangle, so

\[ d^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos\theta. \]

Compute:

  • \(\cos(2.1^\circ) = \cos(0.036652\,\mathrm{rad}) \approx 0.9993283938\).
  • \(d = \sqrt{370^2 + 350^2 - 2(370)(350)\cos(2.1^\circ)} \approx \boxed{23.96\,\mathrm{km}}\).

Numeric result: \(d \approx 23.96\,\mathrm{km}\).

Quick intuition (small-angle check)

For small \(\theta\), \(\cos\theta \approx 1-\tfrac{\theta^2}{2}\) (\(\theta\) in radians). Then

\[ d \approx \sqrt{(a-b)^2 + ab\,\theta^2}. \]

Plugging numbers: \(\theta = 0.036652\,\mathrm{rad}\) gives \(d \approx 23.96\,\mathrm{km}\), very close to the exact value above.
