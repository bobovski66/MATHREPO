Find the distance from station \(A\) to the boat, and the distance of the boat from shore

Two radar stations \(A\) and \(B\) are \(500\) ft apart along the shore. From \(A\) the line of sight to the boat makes a \(70^\circ\) angle with the shore; from \(B\) it makes \(60^\circ\). Find (i) the distance \(AC\) from station \(A\) to the boat, and (ii) the perpendicular distance of the boat from the shore.

Triangle setup

Let \(AB=500\ \mathrm{ft}\). Then the triangle \(\\triangle ABC\) has angles \(\angle A=70^\circ\), \(\angle B=60^\circ\), so \(\angle C = 180^\circ-70^\circ-60^\circ = 50^\circ\).

Law of Sines

With the usual notation \(a=BC\) (opposite \(A\)), \(b=AC\) (opposite \(B\)), \(c=AB=500\) (opposite \(C\)), the Law of Sines gives

\[\frac{a}{\sin 70^\circ}=\frac{b}{\sin 60^\circ}=\frac{c}{\sin 50^\circ}.\]

  • \(b = \dfrac{c\,\sin 60^\circ}{\sin 50^\circ} \approx \boxed{565.26\ \mathrm{ft}}\) â€” distance from station \(A\) to the boat.

Distance from the boat to the shore

The shortest distance from the boat to the shore (line \(AB\)) is the altitude \(h\) from \(C\) to \(AB\). Using right-triangle trigonometry with side \(AC=b\) and angle at \(A\) equal to \(70^\circ\):

\[ h = b\sin 70^\circ \approx \boxed{531.17\ \mathrm{ft}}. \]

Answer: \(AC \approx 565.26\ \mathrm{ft}\), and the boat is \(h \approx 531.17\ \mathrm{ft}\) from the shore.
