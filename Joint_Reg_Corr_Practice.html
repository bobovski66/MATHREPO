Problem 1: Finding the Regression Line and Correlation

A teacher records the number of online practice sessions each student completes before a quiz, and their quiz score (out of 10). The data for five students are:

Student $x_i$ (sessions) $y_i$ (quiz score)
113
224
336
445
557
  1. Compute the sample means $\bar x$ and $\bar y$.
  2. Compute $s_x^2$, $s_y^2$, and the sample covariance $s_{xy}$.
  3. Compute the sample correlation coefficient $r = \dfrac{s_{xy}}{s_x s_y}$.
  4. Find the least squares regression line $\hat y = a + bx$.
  5. Interpret the slope $b$ in context.
  6. Compute $R^2$ and briefly interpret it.
Step 1: Compute $\bar x$ and $\bar y$

Compute the means:

$\displaystyle \bar x = \frac{1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5}{5} = \frac{15}{5} = 3. $

$\displaystyle \bar y = \frac{3 + 4 + 6 + 5 + 7}{5} = \frac{25}{5} = 5. $

Step 2: Compute $s_x^2$, $s_y^2$, and $s_{xy}$

Make a table of deviations, squares, and products:

$i$ $x_i$ $y_i$ $x_i - \bar x$ $y_i - \bar y$ $(x_i - \bar x)^2$ $(y_i - \bar y)^2$ $(x_i - \bar x)(y_i - \bar y)$
113 $1 - 3 = -2$ $3 - 5 = -2$ $(-2)^2 = 4$ $(-2)^2 = 4$ $(-2)(-2) = 4$
224 $2 - 3 = -1$ $4 - 5 = -1$ $(-1)^2 = 1$ $(-1)^2 = 1$ $(-1)(-1) = 1$
336 $3 - 3 = 0$ $6 - 5 = 1$ $0^2 = 0$ $1^2 = 1$ $0 \cdot 1 = 0$
445 $4 - 3 = 1$ $5 - 5 = 0$ $1^2 = 1$ $0^2 = 0$ $1 \cdot 0 = 0$
557 $5 - 3 = 2$ $7 - 5 = 2$ $2^2 = 4$ $2^2 = 4$ $2 \cdot 2 = 4$
Sums: $10$ $10$ $9$

Now compute the variances:

$\displaystyle s_x^2 = \frac{10}{n-1} = \frac{10}{4} = 2.5, \quad s_y^2 = \frac{10}{4} = 2.5. $

So $s_x = s_y = \sqrt{2.5} \approx 1.58$.

The sample covariance is

$\displaystyle s_{xy} = \frac{9}{n-1} = \frac{9}{4} = 2.25. $

Step 3: Compute the correlation $r$

Use the formula $\displaystyle r = \frac{s_{xy}}{s_x s_y}$:

$\displaystyle r = \frac{2.25}{(\sqrt{2.5})(\sqrt{2.5})} = \frac{2.25}{2.5} = 0.9. $

So $r = 0.9$, which indicates a strong positive linear association between practice sessions and quiz score.

Step 4: Find the regression line $\hat y = a + bx$

The slope can be computed from $\displaystyle b = \frac{s_{xy}}{s_x^2}$:

$\displaystyle b = \frac{2.25}{2.5} = 0.9. $

Then the intercept is

$\displaystyle a = \bar y - b\,\bar x = 5 - 0.9 \cdot 3 = 5 - 2.7 = 2.3. $

So the least squares regression line is

$\displaystyle \hat y = 2.3 + 0.9\,x. $

Step 5: Interpret the slope

The slope $b = 0.9$ means:

For each additional practice session, the model predicts an average increase of about $0.9$ points on the quiz (out of 10).

This describes the average trend; individual students can still be above or below the line.

Step 6: Compute and interpret $R^2$

In simple linear regression (one $X$ and one $Y$), $\displaystyle R^2 = r^2$.

$\displaystyle R^2 = r^2 = (0.9)^2 = 0.81. $

Interpretation:

About $81\%$ of the variability in quiz scores can be explained by a straight-line relationship with the number of practice sessions. The remaining $19\%$ is due to other factors or random variation.

Problem 2: Using Correlation to Find the Slope

In another class, a teacher studies the relationship between hours of sleep the night before an exam ($X$) and the exam score ($Y$). For a sample of $n = 20$ students, the computer output reports:

  • Sample mean hours of sleep: $\bar x = 6.5$
  • Sample mean exam score: $\bar y = 78$
  • Sample standard deviation of $X$: $s_x = 1.2$ hours
  • Sample standard deviation of $Y$: $s_y = 8.0$ points
  • Sample correlation: $r = 0.6$
  1. Use $r$, $s_x$, and $s_y$ to find the slope $b$ of the least squares regression line $\hat y = a + bx$.
  2. Find the intercept $a$.
  3. Write the regression equation for predicting exam score from hours of sleep.
  4. Interpret the slope $b$ in context.
  5. Compute $R^2$ and explain what it means in this context.
Step 1: Find the slope $b$ using $r$

In simple linear regression, the slope can be written as

$\displaystyle b = r\,\frac{s_y}{s_x}. $

Plug in the values:

$\displaystyle b = 0.6 \cdot \frac{8.0}{1.2} = 0.6 \cdot \frac{8}{1.2}. $

Compute $\dfrac{8}{1.2}$: $\displaystyle \frac{8}{1.2} \approx 6.67$ (since $1.2 \cdot 6.67 \approx 8$).

Then

$\displaystyle b \approx 0.6 \cdot 6.67 \approx 4.00. $

So the slope is approximately $b = 4.0$.

Step 2: Find the intercept $a$

The regression line passes through $(\bar x, \bar y)$, so

$\displaystyle a = \bar y - b\,\bar x. $

Plug in the values:

$\displaystyle a = 78 - 4.0 \cdot 6.5 = 78 - 26 = 52. $

Step 3: Write the regression equation

The least squares regression line for predicting exam score from hours of sleep is

$\displaystyle \hat y = 52 + 4.0\,x, $

where $x$ is the number of hours of sleep the night before the exam and $\hat y$ is the predicted exam score.

Step 4: Interpret the slope $b$

The slope $b = 4.0$ means:

For each additional hour of sleep, the model predicts an average increase of about 4 points on the exam.

This describes the linear trend in the data; it does not by itself prove that more sleep causes higher scores.

Step 5: Compute and interpret $R^2$

In simple linear regression,

$\displaystyle R^2 = r^2 = (0.6)^2 = 0.36. $

So $R^2 = 0.36$, or $36\%$.

Interpretation:

About $36\%$ of the variability in exam scores can be explained by a straight-line relationship with hours of sleep the night before the exam. The remaining $64\%$ of the variability is due to other factors or random variation.
